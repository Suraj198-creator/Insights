What's happening:
The People's Bank of China (PBOC) has brought the country's dominant payment platforms, Alipay and Tenpay (WeChat Pay), along with clearinghouse NetsUnion, under its direct anti-money laundering (AML) supervision. This means they will now be subject to the same stringent AML regulations as traditional banks.

Why it matters:
This move marks a major regulatory escalation, closing a long-standing loophole and leveling the playing field between FinTech giants and incumbent banks in China. It signals the end of the 'light-touch' regulatory era for big tech in the country's financial sector.

Key Insight:
As digital payments become systemic, regulators globally are shifting focus from simply enabling innovation to managing its risks. This decision in China is a powerful precedent, indicating that at-scale FinTechs will inevitably face bank-level scrutiny and compliance costs.
